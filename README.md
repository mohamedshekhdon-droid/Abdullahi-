## Objectives
1. Conduct detailed regional geology analysis of the Malay Basin
2. Perform well correlation across 3 wells (WC, AW1, AW2)
3. Determine reservoir lithology and fluid contacts
4. Calculate petrophysical properties (porosity, permeability, water saturation)
5. Identify uncertainties and recommend additional data acquisition

---

## Field Overview
- **Location:** Offshore Terengganu, Peninsular Malaysia
- **Water Depth:** 70 feet
- **Field Area:** 96.25 km²
- **Structure Type:** Simple anticline trap
- **Discovery:** January 2018 (Wildcat well)
- **Wells Drilled:** 3 (1 exploration + 2 appraisal)

### Regional Geology
- **Basin:** Malay Basin (500 km × 250 km, NW-trending)
- **Tectonic Setting:** Intracratonic basin formed during Early Tertiary collision of Indian and Eurasian plates
- **Stratigraphy:** Tertiary sediments (up to 14 km thick)
- **Depositional Environment:** Fluvial to tidally-dominated estuarine sands (Early-Middle Miocene)

### 2. Lithology Determination
**Methods Applied:**
- Density-Neutron crossplot analysis
- M-N crossplot validation
- Sand-Silt-Clay (SSC) model

**Lithology Indicators:**
- Bulk Density: 2.0-2.3 g/cc → **Sandstone**
- GR: Low values (clean sand)
- SP: High deflection (permeable zone)

### 3. Petrophysical Analysis

#### Porosity Calculation
**Density-Neutron Average Method:****Parameters:**
- Matrix density (ρ_ma): 2.65 g/cc (sandstone)
- Fluid density (ρ_f): 1.0 g/cc (fresh mud)
- Shale volume (V_sh): From GR index

#### Permeability Calculation
**Core-to-Log Correlation:**Where K = permeability (md), φ = effective porosity (fraction)

**Validation:**
- Core vs. calculated match: <1% difference
- R² correlation: >0.95

#### Water Saturation Modeling
**Leverett J-Function:****Inputs:**
- Capillary pressure (Pc) from core analysis
- Interfacial tension (σ): 30 dynes/cm
- Contact angle (θ): 0° (water-wet)

## Key Findings

### Structural Interpretation
- **Trap Type:** Anticlinal closure
- **Top Sand Depth:** 3,000 ft (WC location)
- **Reservoir Thickness:** 35 ft (net pay across all wells)
- **OWC Depth:** 3,428 ft
- **Closure Area:** ~96 km²

### Reservoir Properties

| Property | Range | Average | Quality |
|----------|-------|---------|---------|
| **Porosity** | 18-27% | 22% | Good to Excellent |
| **Permeability** | 100-266 md | 183 md | Good |
| **Water Saturation** | 25-48% | 35% | - |
| **Oil Saturation** | 52-75% | 65% | - |
| **Net-to-Gross** | - | 1.0 | Clean sand |

### Fluid Contacts
**Evidence from Multiple Methods:**
1. **Log Analysis:** Resistivity separation at 3,428 ft
2. **Pressure Gradient:** 
   - Oil gradient: 0.294 psi/ft
   - Water gradient: 0.456 psi/ft
   - Intersection: 3,428 ft
3. **Well Results:**
   - WC: Oil zone (3,000-3,035 ft)
   - AW1: Penetrated OWC at 3,428 ft
   - AW2: Water zone (drilled below OWC)

### Sand Distribution
- **Northern Flank:** Thinner sand development
- **Southern Flank:** Better sand quality and thickness
- **Interpretation:** Progradational system with preferential sediment transport to south

## Uncertainties & Recommendations

### Data Limitations
- Limited seismic data (structural uncertainty)
- Only 3 wells (sparse spatial coverage)
- No special core analysis (relative permeability, wettability)

### Recommended Actions
1. Acquire 3D seismic for better structural definition
2. Drill additional appraisal wells on flanks
3. Conduct SCAL program for dynamic modeling
4. Pressure transient testing for permeability validation
5. Extended well testing for productivity assessment
